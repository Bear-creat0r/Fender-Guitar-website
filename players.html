Top Ten Fender Guitar Players

Jimi Hendrix

A revolutionary guitarist known for his expressive Stratocaster work and pioneering use of feedback and wah to shape modern rock guitar tone.

Eric Clapton

A blues-influenced virtuoso whose melodic phrasing and long association with Fender Stratocasters helped define rock and blues guitar for generations.

David Gilmour

Pink Floyd's atmospheric lead guitarist, celebrated for his lyrical solos, warm Strat tones, and tasteful use of delay and sustain.

Jeff Beck

An innovative player who pushed the Stratocaster into new stylistic territory with expressive bends, touch techniques, and genre-bending instrumental albums.

Buddy Guy

A Chicago blues legend whose fiery playing, dynamic phrasing, and showmanship influenced rock and blues players across decades.

Stevie Ray Vaughan

A powerhouse blues-rock guitarist known for his raw tone, fierce attack, and soulful Stratocaster work that revived mainstream interest in electric blues.

Mark Knopfler

Dire Straits frontman famed for his fingerpicked, economical style and clean Strat tones that brought storytelling and nuance to rock guitar.

George Harrison

The Beatles' lead guitarist whose tasteful melodies and use of Fender instruments contributed enduring hooks and sonic color to pop and rock.

Ritchie Blackmore

A hard rock pioneer who combined classical-influenced riffs with aggressive Strat work, helping define the sound of Deep Purple and Rainbow.

John Mayer

A contemporary player blending blues, pop, and soul; known for silky Strat tones, tasteful phrasing, and modern signature instruments.
